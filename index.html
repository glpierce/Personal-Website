About Grant

Background

Grant Pierce was born on December 19th, 1996 in New York City, NY. His younger sister, Avery, was born 14 months later and in the summer of 1998, Grant and his family moved to Greenwich, CT. In 2000 Grant's younger brother Cole was born.

In the fall of 2003 Grant began first grade at Brunswick School, where he attended until his high school graduation in 2015. While at Brunswick, Grant served as president of the Wounded Warrior club, played varsity water polo, and was elected captain of the varsity swim team for his senior year.

After high school, Grant enrolled at Stanford University where he earned his BA in Philosophy with a minor in German Studies upon graduating in 2019. At Stanford, Grant continued to play water polo for Stanford's club team and had the opportunity to study abroad in Berlin where he honed his German language skills.

In the summer of 2019, Grant began his professional career at Goldman Sachs as a product manager for the recently launched Apple Card, Goldman and Apple's cobranded credit card. In late 2020, Grant left Goldman to road trip around the United States and initiate a career change. In the fall of 2021, Grant moved to San Francisco, CA and is now enrolled in the Software Engineering program at the Flatiron School.


Interests

Grant is an avid outdoorsman who enjoys hiking and camping and has a particular fondness for mountainous terrain. Grant has visited over 25 national parks and numerous national monuments and national forests. He is also an expert skier and worked as a ski instructor at Stratton Mountain in Vermont while in high school.

In addition to his love of the outdoors, Grant also enjoys travel, roadtrips, and exploration. Grant enjoys international travel, when possible, as it affords him the opportunity to learn about other countries, cultures, histories, languages, and religions. Amongst those who know him, Grant has a reputation as a prolific long distance driver who never turns down an opportunity to get behind the wheel.

Beyond his new area of study, software engineering, Grant has strong interest in philosophy, geography, history, economics, linguistics, and religion/spirituality.
